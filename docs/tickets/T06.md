## Goal

Subsample benign calibration scores at `n_cal ∈ {50, 100, 250, 500, 1000, 5000}` with 100 fixed-seed repeats per cell (30 minimum). Report median and IQR over repeats. This is the primary empirical defense against the "FPR equalized by construction" critique.

## Why This Exists

Reviewers will argue that B2 per-client thresholds trivially equalize FPR by construction. The calibration-size sweep tests whether B2 thresholds generalize beyond the calibration set. This is the primary tautology defense.

## Scope

- For each `n_cal` in grid, subsample benign calibration scores per client
- Apply B2 threshold at each subsample
- Evaluate FPR on held-out test_benign data
- 100 fixed-seed repeats per (client, seed, n_cal)
- Report median and IQR of CV(FPR) over repeats
- Produce sensitivity table and curve

## Out of Scope

- Retraining at different calibration sizes
- Changing the eligibility threshold (n_min=100 remains fixed for reporting)
- Clients with fewer than n_cal samples are excluded from that n_cal level

## Implementation Requirements

2. n_cal grid: `{50, 100, 250, 500, 1000, 5000}` from config
3. Repeat count: 100 (from config; minimum 30)

5. Aggregate: median and IQR of CV(FPR) over repeats per n_cal
6. Determinism: same seeds produce same subsamples
7. Output: sensitivity table (Parquet) + curve figure
